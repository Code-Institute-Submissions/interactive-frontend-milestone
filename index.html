A Guide to Dubai

Even if you’ve never been to Dubai before, you’ve probably heard something about it. It’s a city that breaks records all the time. It’s home to everything from the world’s tallest building to the world’s largest sweet shop. If you’re planning on visiting Dubai, though, you might want to know about the best places to go. And if that’s the case, then you’re in luck. This website has all the information you need to know about Dubai’s top ten tourist attractions, from the Burj Khalifa to IMG Worlds of Adventure.

The number of people visiting Dubai increases every year, making the emirate one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world. From January to June of 2019, for example, more than 8 million tourists holidayed in Dubai. The number’s only going to increase in 2020, with the launch of Expo 2020 – there are estimated to be around 20 million visitors to Dubai this year.

While many people visit Dubai, not a lot of people know their way round. People know about landmarks such as the Burj Khalifa or the Burj Al Arab, but it’s difficult to get a sense of how the city is laid out, particularly because the heat means you can’t walk around outside. Instead, people make use of the city’s taxis, buses, or the Dubai Metro. This website aims to show visitors how Dubai is laid out, and where exactly the city’s top landmarks are. Read on to find out more.

Dubai's Top 10 Landmarks

Information

About: At 2,711 feet, the Burj Khalifa is the tallest building in the world, and the UAE’s most iconic building. Visitors can head to At The Top, an outdoor observation deck on the 124th floor, to check out the view. Up on the 148th floor is the SKY Lounge, which is one of the world’s highest observation decks.

Tickets: From AED149

Opening Hours: 9am-11pm

Website: www.burjkhalifa.ae

About: Dubai’s most iconic hotel, the Burj Al Arab first opened in 1999. It’s built on a manmade island, and was designed to look like the sail of a ship. As well as being one of the tallest hotels in the world, it’s also one of the most luxurious – and most expensive.

Tickets: n/a

Opening Hours: n/a

Website: www.jumeirah.com/en/stay/dubai/burj-al-arab-jumeirah

About: The Dubai Frame is exactly what the name implies, and holds the record for the largest frame in the world. It’s 150 metres tall, with an observation deck up at the top. Seen from one side, it frames the skyline of old Dubai, while from the other side, you can see the more modern landmarks of the city.

Tickets: AED50 for adults, AED20 for children 3-12, free for children under 3

Opening Hours: 9am-9pm

Website: wwww.dubaiframe.ae

About: Ski Dubai is an indoor ski slope in the heart of one of Dubai’s largest malls, the Mall of the Emirates. The slope is the same height as a 25-storey building, and there are five slopes of varying difficulty on it. There’s also a snow park at the foot of the slope, with slides and snowballs, and a chance to interact with penguins.

Tickets: AED210-490 to go skiing, AED190-490 to visit the snow park, AED230-340 to see the penguins.

Opening Hours: 10am-11pm Sunday to Wednesday, 10am-12am Thursday , 9am-11pm Friday to Saturday

Website: www.skidxb.com

About: Alserkal Avenue is a collection of warehouses that has become the heart of Dubai’s arts and culture scene. It’s home to more than 20 different art galleries, as well as design studios, exhibition spaces, cafes, shops, theatres, and more. It regularly hosts cultural events throughout the year.

Tickets: n/a

Opening Hours: All galleries have different opening hours

Website: www.alserkalavenue.ae

About: La Mer is a beachfront destination close to the heart of the city. Head to the beach for a spot of sunbathing, or some retail therapy, or dine at one of La Mer’s many restaurants and cafés. It’s even got it’s own waterpark, and plenty of street art murals to admire.

Tickets: n/a

Opening Hours: 10am-10pm Sunday to Wednesday, 10am-12am Thursday to Saturday

Website: www.lamerdubai.ae

About: One of Dubai’s largest waterparks, Aquaventure can be found at the top of the Palm Jumeirah. The waterpark’s full of rides, beaches, swimming pools, and play areas, as well as chances to get up close and personal with marine animals such as sharks, dolphins, sea lions, and rays.

Tickets: AED320 for adults, AED260 for children, free for children under 2

Opening Hours: 10am-sunset

Website: www.atlantis.com/dubai

About: With over 50 million flowers, Dubai Miracle Garden is the world’s largest natural flower garden. It’s full of flower sculptures and displays, including a replica of an Airbus A380 aeroplane. It’s also home to an indoor butterfly garden, which houses over 15,000 butterflies.

Tickets: AED55 for adults, AED40 for children 3-12, free for children under 3

Opening Hours: 9am-9pm Sunday to Thursday, 9am-11pm Friday, 9am-10pm Saturday

Website: www.dubaimiraclegarden.com

About: Dubai Opera is the first theatre of its kind to open in Dubai, and was designed to resemble a traditional dhow. It first opened in 2016, with a performance by Placido Domingo. It has 2,000 seats, and has hosted everything from operas and musicals to stand-up comedy gigs and squash tournaments.

Tickets: Free to visit, prices of shows will vary

Opening Hours: Box office is open from 10am-9pm

Website: www.dubaiopera.com

About: One of the world’s largest indoor theme parks, IMG Worlds of Adventure is spread out over 1.5 million square feet. The park first opened in 2016, and is split up into five separate zones, with rides, restaurants and shops based on characters from Marvel, Cartoon Network, and more.

Tickets: AED 299, AED175 for adults over 60

Opening Hours: 11am-8pm Saturday to Wednesday, 11am-10pm Thursday to Friday

Website: www.imgworlds.com
